TECHNICAL_METHODOLOGY

Technical Methodology.

RAMAN-LINK is an interface designed to reduce the manual physical and mental effort involved in spectral interpretation. It provides an automated framework for assignment based on validated physical standards.

01. INSTRUMENT AGNOSTICISM

Our engine utilizes a Universal Heuristic Parser to handle diverse laboratory data formats. It automatically tunnels through metadata headers, detects unit conflicts (nm vs cm⁻¹), and normalizes detector intensity to a 0-1 scale.

02. METADATA EXTRACTION

Automation of literature search and reference marker identification. This engine correlates research metadata with raw data to provide initial interpretation markers.

03. VIBRATIONAL PERTURBATION MODEL

Calculation of energy shifts between molecular states and solid-state environments using established physical laws (Hydrogen Bonding, Steric Strain).

04. STANDARDIZED DATA PROCESSING

A deterministic mathematical sequence for signal preparation: Modified Z-Score despiking, 5th-order polynomial baseline correction, and Savitzky-Golay smoothing.

05. THE SOCRATES STANDARD

Structural correlation grounded in the Socrates Standard—the characteristic group frequency tables of George Socrates. This enables evidence-based assignments backed by peer-reviewed spectroscopic truth.

06. THE MISSION: COGNITIVE REDUCTION

The system is designed to automate the foundational 10-20% of manual data preparation and initial assignment, allowing the researcher to focus on higher-level analysis.
